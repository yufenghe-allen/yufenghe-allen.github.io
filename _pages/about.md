I am Yufeng Allen He, a Ph.D. candidate in Integrated Life Sciences (Physics) at the Academy for Advanced Interdisciplinary Studies, Peking University.

My research lies at the intersection of computational biology, cancer biology, and **artificial intelligence**. Broadly, I am interested in developing rigorous computational frameworks to understand complex biological systems across scales, from molecular programs and cellular states to tissue architecture and disease microenvironments. My work has focused on spatial multi-omics, tumor immunology, and **deep generative modeling**, with an emphasis on integrating multimodal biological data and extracting biologically meaningful structure from high-dimensional measurements.

More broadly, I aim to build an ambitious research program in academia that bridges omics, pathology, imaging, and clinical phenotypes through **foundation models**, **generative AI**, and **multimodal** learning, and to develop generalizable computational systems for biological discovery and translational medicine. In particular, I am highly interested in the integration of **generative AI** with clinically grounded research in tumor biology, aging biology, and neurobiology. My long-term goal is to establish an independent academic career centered on next-generation biomedical intelligence, spanning **computational pathology**, **cross-modal representation learning**, **multimodal generative models**, and clinically informed AI systems.

I am currently seeking postdoctoral opportunities and plan to continue developing my career in academia. As the traditional boundary between omics research and clinical medicine is increasingly being reshaped by rapid advances in **computational pathology**, **biomedical agents**, and **multimodal generative models**, I hope to devote my postdoctoral training over the next three years to integrating more diverse omics modalities with clinical and imaging-derived data, and to developing the next generation of general-purpose medical AI agents. In this process, I am especially interested in advancing **biomedical foundation models**, **scalable pretraining**, **long-context reasoning**, and **interpretability** for complex real-world biomedical data.
